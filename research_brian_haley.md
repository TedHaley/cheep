# Brian Haley - Research Summary

## Background

**Brian Carlo Haley** (born February 12, 1963) is an American actor and comedian. He was born in **Seattle, Washington** into a large Catholic family as the **fifth of six children**. His father was an airline executive and his mother was a homemaker and part-time maid. His father is of **Irish and Italian descent**, which is where he gets his Italian middle name, Carlo.

At the age of three, his family moved to **Saint-Jovite, Quebec, Canada**, where he was put in a boarding home. During this 18-month stay, he learned to speak **fluent French**. He disliked the experience so much that upon returning to Seattle, he refused to speak the language except to translate for his younger sister, who only spoke French.

As a child, he began acting in the Seattle area through community and school theater. As a teenager, he stumbled upon the film set of *Scorchy* (1976) in downtown Seattle. After seeing some of the movie being filmed, jumping into a few shots as an extra, and meeting the star **Connie Stevens**, he decided to pursue a career in show business. At age 15, he moved to rural **Ellensburg, Washington**.

After high school, he took on several blue-collar jobs, including **lumberjack**, **hay buck**, and **ranch hand**. He eventually enlisted in the **U.S. Army** with the goal of joining the **Green Berets (Army Special Forces)**. He served from **1980 to 1985**, where he won letters of commendation and the **Army Achievement Medal**, but left early to pursue show business. While in the Army (in 1983), he appeared in a production of *Guys and Dolls* at the **Ft. Bragg Playhouse** starring **Joe Namath**.

## Professional Work

### Stand-Up Comedy Career
After his tour in the Army, Haley began doing **stand-up comedy** in Seattle and quickly rose to **headliner status**. In **1988**, he moved to **Los Angeles** where he had immediate success:

- Won the **"Hollywood's Hottest New Comic"** competition
- Appeared on several stand-up comedy TV shows including *An Evening At The Improv*
- Was picked up by **ABC Television** for a one-year holding deal
- Appeared as a contestant on *Classic Concentration* (hosted by Alex Trebek) in July 1988, winning **$11,285** in prizes

His proverbial **big break** came with an appearance on ***The Tonight Show Starring Johnny Carson*** in **1990**, which launched his career in earnest. His first Carson appearance led to a flood of television and film roles, as well as an **HBO comedy special**.

### Film Career (Notable Roles)
Haley's stand-up act is characterized by playing his **"all-American looks against manic outbursts and absurd situations."** He has appeared in numerous films:

- ***Baby's Day Out*** (1994) – Victor "Veeko" Riley (the incompetent kidnapper). The movie was hugely popular in overseas markets, especially **India**, where it played at the largest theater in **Calcutta** for over a year and was remade with an Indian cast as *Sisindri*.
- ***Little Giants*** (1994) – Mike Hammersmith ("Spike's Dad"), the over-the-top football father
- ***Mars Attacks!*** (1996) – Mitch, Secret Service Agent
- ***That Darn Cat*** (1997) – Marvin
- ***McHale's Navy*** (1997 film) – Christy
- ***The Man Who Wasn't There*** (2001) – Officer Krebs (Coen Brothers film)
- ***Pearl Harbor*** (2001) – Training Captain
- ***The Departed*** (2006) – Detective #2 Tailing Queenan (Martin Scorsese)
- ***Gran Torino*** (2008) – **Mitch Kowalski** (Clint Eastwood's discontented son; award-winning film)
- ***The Taking of Pelham 1 2 3*** (2009) – Police Captain Hill (MTA) (Tony Scott)
- ***The Adjustment Bureau*** (2011) – Officer Maes
- ***Draft Day*** (2014) – NFL Commissioner (uncredited)
- ***The Amazing Spider-Man 2*** (2014) – Phil Watson (scenes deleted)
- ***Courting Des Moines*** (2016) – Sec. Todd Voss
- ***Law & Order*** (2022) – Senator Alan Chandler

### Television Career
- ***Wings*** (1995–1996, NBC) – Replaced **Thomas Haden Church** playing mechanic **Budd Bronski** (Season 7)
- ***Maggie Winters*** (1998–1999, CBS) – Bartender Tom Vanderhulst
- Guest appearances on: *30 Rock*, *The Drew Carey Show*, *ER*, *The Hughleys*, *Third Watch*, *Law & Order: Criminal Intent*
- *Beyond Belief: Fact or Fiction* (1998) – Firefighter Roger Parks in "Fire Station 32"
- *The Weird Al Show* (1997) – "The Hooded Avenger"
- NFL Blitz video game commentary (along with Tim Kitzrow)

### Broadway
In **February 2007**, Haley appeared on **Broadway** as tennis commentator **Ryan Becker** in the **Terrence McNally** play ***Deuce***, directed by **Michael Blakemore**.

### Other Work
- In 1995, starred in a **Clio Award-winning Super Bowl ad** for **Miller Lite** beer playing hard-luck football quarterback Elmer Bruker (a man who was on every winning Super Bowl team but never played)
- Has a production company called **Haleywood Productions**
- He owns the **Frank Sinatra booth** and several other items from **Chasen's** restaurant
